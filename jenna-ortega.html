Jenna Ortega: Biography, Actor, Wednesday Star

Who Is Jenna Ortega?

Actor Jenna Ortega plays the lead role in the hit Netflix show Wednesday. Ortega, who is of Mexican and Puerto Rican heritage, has been acting since she was 9 years old. She successfully transitioned beyond her early Disney Channel stardom with roles in several horror films, such as X and Studio 666. In this decade’s reboot of the Scream franchise, the bonafide scream queen plays Tara Carpenter. Her performance as Wednesday Addams earned her a 2023 Emmy nomination for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Comedy Series.

Early Life

Born on September 27, 2002, in La Quinta, California, Jenna Marie Ortega has been acting since she was 9 years old when she landed her very first gig starring in a Colgate toothpaste commercial. Her parents, Edward and Natalie, supported Ortega’s dream of becoming an actor, though they “have no interest whatsoever in [Hollywood] or in the film industry,” Ortega said in an Elle interview.

Still, Ortega is grateful for the support she receives from her family saying, “they sacrificed a lot for me to do what I do.” Before she became famous, Ortega’s mom would drive her from their family home in California’s Coachella Valley to Los Angeles for auditions, a trip that would take hours of driving each way.

Natalie is an emergency room nurse, and Edward works in the California district attorney’s office. Ortega, who is of Mexican and Puerto Rican heritage, is one of six siblings.

Movies and TV Shows: 'Wednesday,' 'You,' 'Iron Man 3,' and More

Courtesy of Netflix

Jenna Ortega stars in Netflix’s series Wednesday.

After a brief appearance in Iron Man 3, Ortega hit her stride with several large roles including a recurring one in The CW television show Jane the Virgin, the Netflix series You, the main character in Disney’s Stuck in the Middle, and the most recent entries in the Scream film franchise as Tara Carpenter. Her onscreen success wasn’t always the case, however, and Ortega has been vocal about having a hard time landing roles at times because she felt that casting directors didn’t think she had “the look they were going for.” Still, Ortega’s dedication to acting paid off as she began landing bigger and bigger roles in the mainstream media.

Ortega’s largest role to date is as the lead in Netflix’s Wednesday, in which she portrays the iconic Wednesday Addams in a modern, live-action take on the beloved The Addams Family series. Ortega’s portrayal of the titular character earned her Emmy, Golden Globe, and Screen Actors Guild Awards nominations as well as an award for Best Performance in a Show at the 2023 MTV Movie and TV Awards. Other Hollywood heavyweights appeared alongside Ortega in Wednesday including Catherine Zeta-Jones, Luis Guzmán, and Ortega’s beloved offscreen friend Gwendoline Christie. In the show, Christie plays Principal Weems and towers over the 5-foot-1-inch-tall Ortega with her 6-foot-3 stature.

Dive Deeper

The former Disney child actor has also become somewhat of a scream queen, having landed several roles in slasher films including X, Insidious: Chapter 2, and The Babysitter: Killer Queen. Ortega has also joined the long-running Scream franchise as a cast member in the reboot films.

Jane The Virgin

Ortega portrayed a young Jane Villanueva, alongside the adult version of the titular character played by Gina Rodriguez, across several episodes of this hit show on The CW. In flashbacks, Ortega depicts Jane as she maneuvers major obstacles in her day-to-day life. Ortega reprised this role across 30 episodes from 2014 to 2019.

Stuck in the Middle

Ortega played middle-child and aspiring inventor Harley Diaz in Disney’s Stuck in the Middle. Ortega starred in the series starting in 2016 until its cancellation in 2018. Stuck in the Middle wasn’t Ortega’s only Disney role; she also voiced Princess Isabel in the animated Elena of Avalor series.

You

In season 2 of Netflix’s You, Ortega played Ellie Alves, the younger sister of a reporter who lives in the same building as obsessive serial killer Joe. Fans and showrunners alike enjoyed Ortega’s performance as Ellie so much that she was invited to return for season 4 but ultimately had to turn the offer down due to scheduling conflicts while filming Wednesday.

The Fallout

Ortega starred as a survivor of a mass school shooting in Max’s 2021 film The Fallout. Ortega’s performance was highly praised as Vada, a high schooler forced to deal with moving through the aftermath, trauma, and grief of the event.

Scream (2022) and Scream VI

Ortega portrays high schooler Tara Carpenter in Scream (2022) and recreates the famous opening scene in which a serial killer named Ghostface taunts her after she picks up a call from an unknown number. Ortega’s role in this movie—especially after her parts in other horror flicks like Insidious: Chapter 2 and the gore-filled X, which came out on the heels of 2022’s Scream—began to cement her status as a slasher film mainstay. Scream VI was released in March 2023 and saw Ortega reprise the role of final girl Tara Carpenter.

Wednesday

Ortega stars in the Netflix hit series Wednesday, which was the most watched premiere of 2022. Ortega’s nuanced performance as Wednesday Addams was widely praised and helped secure a second season.

In the show, Ortega is enrolled at a school full of paranormal (and paranormal-adjacent) students after being kicked out of a regular public school because she intentionally maimed the swim team. Once Wednesday’s settled into her new digs–which she hates–she finds herself at the center of several mysteries and resolves to discover the truth. Ortega was hands-on with this character, making changes to scripts and even choreographing the much-loved dance sequence seen in the first season’s fourth episode.

At 20 years old, Ortega has also made waves with Wednesday on account of her 2023 Emmy nomination for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Comedy. According to The Hollywood Reporter, this makes her the second youngest leading actress nominee in a comedic role after Patty Duke’s 1964 nomination at age 18

Beetlejuice 2

Nearly four decades after directing the cult-classic Beetlejuice, Tim Burton is getting behind the camera to direct the film’s sequel, Beetlejuice 2. Ortega, who previously worked with Burton on Wednesday, will star in the flick as Lydia Deetz’s daughter alongside Winona Ryder and Micheal Keaton, who are confirmed to reprise their roles. Beetlejuice 2 is slated for a September 6, 2024, theatrical release.

Net Worth

Ortega’s net worth is reportedly in the neighborhood of $5 million. However, it’s unclear what Ortega gets paid for certain roles, including her salary for Wednesday.

Personal Life

Ortega is notoriously private about several aspects of her life, including who she dates. Opening up about her dating life in a March 2023 Armchair Expert podcast interview with Dax Shepard, Ortega said she’s not especially interested in dating at the moment because of how demanding her work schedule is and because of the vulnerability a relationship requires.

Ortega also told Elle that the idea of dating stresses her out and that at least for the moment, “I don’t need to think about that right now.” Still, that hasn’t stopped the rumor mill from churning. There’s been speculation that Ortega might have been romantically involved with actor Pete Davidson, but there’s no evidence to substantiate this claim.

She currently lives in Los Angeles.
