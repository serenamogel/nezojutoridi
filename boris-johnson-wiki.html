Boris Johnson Biography, Early Life, Career

Early Life:-  Boris Johnson was born on June 19, 1964, as the eldest of four children to Stanley Johnson and Charlotte Johnson Wahl in New York City, USA. Boris was born with dual citizenship in the United States and the United Kingdom. His family moved to the United Kingdom when he was a child. Boris was sent to 'Ashdown House,' an East Sussex preparatory boarding school where he excelled. He excelled at Ancient Greek and Latin while also developing an interest in rugby.

Professional Career:- In July 2019, he was sworn in as Prime Minister of the United Kingdom. From 2008 until 2016, he was the mayor of London. He was formerly the Member of Parliament (MP) for Henley from 2001 to 2008, and he has been the Member of Parliament (MP) for Uxbridge and South Ruislip since 2015. From 2016 to 2018, he was also Foreign Secretary. Johnson, a member of the 'Conservative Party,' is also a well-known historian and journalist. Born in New York City to English parents, he spent his early life in the United Kingdom. His parents admired great achievers, and he was groomed to be competitive from an early age.He was a bright student who received a 'King's Scholarship' to study at the famous 'Eton College,' after which he went on to study Classics at 'Balliol College,' Oxford.
