Why Won’t there be a Season 3 of The Haunting of Hill House?

Flanagan expressed his happiness in focusing on other aspects of his life and mentioned that it wouldn’t make much sense for the show to continue without his involvement since he is no longer associated with Netflix.

The good news is that The Haunting is an anthology series, so you can delve into the first two seasons without any concerns about unresolved storylines. Flanagan stated, “I’m much happier focusing on other things… and being that I’m no longer at Netflix, that ship has definitively sailed. There will not be a season 3.”

As for Flanagan’s upcoming endeavours, his next project remains a mystery. However, it’s worth noting that he and his production company, Intrepid Pictures, have secured an exclusive first-look TV deal with Amazon Studios. Additionally, Flanagan possesses the television rights to Stephen King’s popular dark fantasy series, The Dark Tower.

The Haunting of Hill House Season 2 Recap:

The Haunting of Bly Manor is a spine-chilling American gothic romance drama miniseries created by Mike Flanagan and released on Netflix on October 9, 2020. It is the second entry in Flanagan’s The Haunting anthology series, following The Haunting of Hill House. The show acts as a detailed adaptation of the 1898 novella The Turn of the Screw by Henry James, but also includes other elements either based on James’ other works or created for the show.

The series follows the chilling events occurring in the eponymous countryside manor in the United Kingdom, mostly upon a young American hired as an au pair for the two children living at Bly. Little does she know, the manor is haunted, and the events that occur will give you nightmares for weeks. The show has been praised for its performances, particularly those of Victoria Pedretti and T’Nia Miller. Be sure to watch it with the lights on!
