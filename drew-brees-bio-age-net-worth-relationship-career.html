Drew Brees is a football star who plays quarterback for the New Orleans Saints of the National Football League (NFL). He began his career as a quarterback at Westlake High School and subsequently went on to play quarterback at Purdue University, where he led the Boilermakers to the Big Ten Championship and a Rose Bowl berth.

Brees was chosen by the San Diego Chargers in 2001 and appeared in his first Pro Bowl with the team in 2004. Later, in 2006, he joined the New Orleans Saints, where he guided the club to Super Bowl XLIV triumph in 2009. Brees holds several NFL records for throwing yards, completions, and touchdowns.

What is the Net Worth of Drew Brees? Salary, Earnings

Drew Brees is one of the greatest quarterbacks in NFL history. During his 19-year career, he has established various records and earned hundreds of millions of dollars.

In March 2020, he agreed to terms with the New Orleans Saints on a two-year, $50 million contract that included a $23 million signing bonus. Throughout his career, he has made more than $244 million.

Where was Drew born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Drew was born on January 15, 1979, in Dallas, Texas. He is the eldest son of trial lawyer Eugene Wilson “Chip” Brees II and lawyer Mina Ruth (née Akins). Reid Brees is his younger sibling.

Drew hails from an athletic family. His father played basketball at Texas A&M, and his mother was an all-state player in three high school sports.

His maternal grandpa, Ray Akins, is regarded as one of the top high school football coaches in Texas history, and his uncle, Marty Akins, was an All-Southwest Conference quarterback in 1975. Reid, his friend, was a Baylor Bears outfielder. His grandpa also took part in the Battle of Okinawa.

His parents divorced and divided custody of the children when he was seven years old. Even though Drew and his brother were going through a difficult period, the scenario brought the brothers together.

As a consequence of their father’s remarriage to Amy Hightower, the daughter of former United States Representative Jack English Hightower, they have a half-sister called Audrey.

Drew enrolled at Westlake High School in 1993. During his high school years, he excelled in basketball, baseball, and football. In the fall of 1996, he captained the team to an undefeated regular-season record and a state title.

Drew attended Purdue University and resumed his football career. During his four years with the Boilermakers, he helped them win the Big Ten Championship and a trip to the Rose Bowl. In 2001, he graduated with a bachelor’s degree in industrial management and was a member of the Sigma Chi fraternity.
